# 17hats — Competitor Analysis

## Overview
17hats is a budget-friendly all-in-one business management platform for solopreneurs, covering quotes, contracts, invoices, scheduling, bookkeeping, and basic automation in a single dashboard. Founded in 2013, it targets the budget-conscious solo operator.

## Features
- Quotes and proposals with conversion to contracts
- Contracts with e-signatures
- Invoicing with recurring billing, payment reminders, online payments (Stripe/Square)
- Scheduling with calendar sync and automated booking
- Basic bookkeeping with income/expense tracking and bank connection
- Questionnaires and intake forms
- Workflow automation (basic — available on higher tiers)
- Client portal
- Lead capture forms
- Templates for documents and emails
- Time tracking ($5/mo add-on)
- QuickBooks sync (Standard tier+)
- Zapier integration
- Mobile app

## UX
17hats is functional but dated. The interface feels a generation behind HoneyBook and Dubsado. Setup is reasonably quick (about an hour). The learning curve is gentler than HubSpot but the UI lacks polish. Workflow follows natural client progression: quote → contract → invoice → payment.

## Pricing
- Essentials: $15/mo — core features, limited send volume
- Standard: $30/mo — higher send limits, scheduling, automation
- Premier: $60/mo — unlimited quotes/invoices, priority support, full features
- Bank Connect: $5/mo add-on
- Additional users/brands: available at extra cost
- 7-day free trial, no permanent free tier

## Strengths
- Most affordable entry price in the category ($15/mo)
- Built-in bookkeeping for simple financial tracking
- Covers all essentials for a solopreneur business
- Straightforward, no-nonsense approach
- In-house support team
- Zapier integration for workflow extension

## Weaknesses
- Interface looks and feels dated compared to modern competitors
- Lower tiers cap document send volume (quotes, invoices)
- Automation is basic — significantly lighter than Dubsado or HoneyBook
- Limited customization compared to Dubsado
- Mobile experience is poor
- Smaller community and fewer learning resources
- No team collaboration features (solopreneur-only)
- Bookkeeping is not a replacement for QuickBooks

## User Complaints
- Dated interface is the most consistent criticism
- Document send limits on Essentials tier feel restrictive
- Automation capabilities are too basic for growing businesses
- Mobile app is nearly unusable for complex tasks
- Limited customization options for client-facing documents
- Add-ons ($5/mo for time tracking, $5/mo for bank connect) inflate the base price

## Review Summary
- MakerStack: 6.8/10 — "functional, affordable tool for solopreneurs"
- Waco3: "adequate tools across categories rather than best-in-class"
- Thrivelance: "practical and affordable rather than slick"
- Capterra: ~3.9/5 — praised for affordability, criticized for dated UI
- GetApp: rated for centralizing invoicing, contracts, and client management
- Best for: budget-conscious solopreneurs; weak for teams or power users

## Overall Assessment
17hats competes primarily on price. Its dated interface, basic automation, and solopreneur-only focus make it vulnerable to a more modern, team-capable alternative. Solo Suite's opportunity: offer a contemporary UI and better automation at a competitive price point.
