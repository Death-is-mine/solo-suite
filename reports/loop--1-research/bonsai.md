# Bonsai — Competitor Analysis

## Overview
Bonsai is an all-in-one freelance business management platform covering proposals, contracts, invoicing, time tracking, CRM, project management, expense tracking, and US tax preparation. Acquired by Zoom in December 2025.

## Features
- Proposals, contracts (attorney-reviewed templates), and e-signatures
- Invoicing with recurring/retainer billing, multi-currency, payment reminders
- Time tracking with start/stop timer and timesheet views
- CRM with pipeline/deal tracking and client profiles
- Project management with Kanban boards, Gantt charts (Premium), task templates
- Expense tracking and bank account syncing
- US tax preparation (Bonsai Tax — $100/year add-on)
- Client portal and branded client forms
- Integrations: Google Calendar, Gmail, Stripe, PayPal (all plans); QuickBooks, Zapier, Slack, Xero (Premium only)

## UX
Bonsai offers a clean, unified interface that flows from proposal to payment. Users consistently report that the proposal-to-invoice pipeline is seamless and reduces admin time significantly. The interface is functional but some reviewers note it feels dated compared to single-purpose modern tools.

## Pricing
- Starter: $9/user/mo (annual) or $15/mo — time tracking, tasks, CRM
- Essentials: $19/user/mo or $25/mo — adds proposals, contracts, invoicing
- Premium: $29/user/mo or $39/mo — adds Gantt, workload, deals pipeline, Zapier/QuickBooks
- Elite: $49/user/mo (3-user min) — custom onboarding, advanced permissions
- Bonsai Tax: $100/year add-on (US only)
- Payments: Stripe and PayPal (processing fees apply)

## Strengths
- Genuinely integrated proposal-to-payment pipeline (workflow continuity)
- Attorney-reviewed contract templates save legal costs
- US tax features are genuinely useful for self-employed filers
- Replaces 4-5 separate tools for solo freelancers
- Unlimited clients and projects on all plans

## Weaknesses
- Per-user pricing scales painfully for teams (5-person team on Premium = $145/mo)
- Project management is basic — limited subtasks, no dependencies on lower tiers
- QuickBooks and Zapier locked behind Premium ($29/user)
- UI feels dated next to modern competitors
- Zoom acquisition (Dec 2025) creates uncertainty about freelancer-first roadmap
- Tax features are US-only; international users get limited value

## User Complaints
- Pricing climbs to $39/mo for full feature set, plus $10/mo for tax
- Each individual tool is "good enough" rather than best-in-class
- No free plan — 7-day trial requires credit card
- Transaction fees on Bonsai Payments add cost on top of subscription
- Mobile app functional but limited for time tracking specifics
- Per-user pricing makes teams expensive fast

## Review Summary
- Usereviews.io: 7.4/10 — "best tool for freelancers who hate admin"
- MakerStack: 7.8/10 — "good enough at everything, great at nothing"
- Capterra: ~4.3/5 — praised for workflow integration, criticized for pricing escalations
- Praised for: proposal-to-payment pipeline, contract templates, tax features
- Criticized for: dated UI, per-user pricing, limited PM depth

## Overall Assessment
Bonsai is the strongest direct competitor for Solo Suite in the freelance CRM space. Its integrated workflow is genuinely useful for solo operators. However, per-user pricing, limited PM features, and Zoom acquisition uncertainty create openings for a better-positioned alternative.
